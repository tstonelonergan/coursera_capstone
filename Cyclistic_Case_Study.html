Cyclistic

In 2016, Cyclistic launched a successful bike-share offering. Since then, the program has grown to a fleet of 5,824 bicycles that are geotracked and locked into a network of 692 stations across Chicago. The bikes can be unlocked from one station and returned to any other station in the system anytime.

Member riders are those who have purchased an annual pass that grants unlimited access to the Cyclistic bike-share system all year round. Casual members have purchased either a single-way ride or a full day pass, granting unlimited access to the bike-share system for a 24 hour period, starting at the time of sale.

Three questions will guide the future marketing program:

  • How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?
  • Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships?
  • How can Cyclistic use digital media to influence casual riders to become members?

Deliverables

Statement of the business task

Cyclistic wants to understand differences between annual riders and casual riders, so that they can piece together why casual riders would convert to annual memberships. Cyclistic has determined that annual members are significantly more profitable than casual riders, so there is a strong business driver to make those conversions. Lastly, they are wanting to target the casual riders by using digital media and are looking at ways to effectively convert more riders into members.

Member riders are likely to be heading toward places of commerce, transit, or education.

Top three recommendations based on analysis

  1. Digital marketing campaign showing the benefits of riding a Cyclistic bike to work or school, particularly targeting the most frequented stations
  2. Black Friday sale on annual membership to attempt to convert casual riders
  3. Explore profitability of a weekly pass and student pass offering

Documentation of any cleaning or manipulation of data

As stated above, I have begun the cleaning process by making copies of all datasets and combined them by year.

Once the data was all in one place, I began cleaning. I created a field ride_length which calculated the duration of a given ride by subtracting the started_at value from the ended_at value. I also created a day_of_week field using the weekdays command, as part of the analysis included comparing characteristics between different days of the week. I also pulled the month value out of the started_at variable so I could review trends on a monthly basis as well.

Using unique() I verified that there were exactly two responses in member_casual, no nulls and no mislabeled data.

I removed any ride_lengths with negative values. I also removed data where all of end_station_name, end_station_id, end_lat, and end_lang were missing. These bikes were removed from circulation by Cyclistic for QA or maintenance. These datapoints were extreme outliers in ride_length and would have affected the overall analysis.
